Postscript's headline pricing event of 2025-2026 was the **structural shift from pure usage-based to monthly platform fee + per-message usage** — Starter from $0/mo + $49 minimum spend, Growth $100/mo, Professional $500/mo, Enterprise custom. The operator response has been visible: documented Shopify App Store quotes calling out the change as material. The structural pricing story most operators still don't fully internalize is the **onramp reset**: minimum spend went from $25 to $49, free trial credit went from $1,000 to $100 (verified 2026-06-03 against [postscript.io/pricing](https://postscript.io/pricing)). Both shifts push entry-stage brands toward later commitment moments.

This piece does the math at the bands where Shopify DTC brands actually run SMS (10K–500K monthly sends) using rates verified 2026-06-03 against the vendor pricing page.

## Bottom line up front

- **Best for:** Shopify DTC brands at $20K+ MRR running SMS as a serious revenue channel — value Shopify-specific depth over multi-channel breadth.
- **Avoid if:** Non-Shopify merchants (Postscript is Shopify-only), brands needing email + SMS + push in one platform (Omnisend covers that), pilots under $20K MRR where platform fees dominate.
- **Standout strength:** 45+ Shopify-specific segmentation filters + built-in TCPA compliance + Shopper AI 1:1 commerce assistant — the depth of Shopify integration is uncontested in the SMS category.
- **Biggest weakness:** The 2025 pricing reset (platform fee + per-message) raised the cost floor for low-volume brands and the minimum-spend / trial-credit changes made the pilot path harder.

## What changed in 2025-2026 (in order of bill impact)

**1. Structural pricing model shift.** Postscript moved from pure usage-based (pay per message only) to platform fee + per-message. The new tier ladder, verified 2026-06-03:

| Tier | Platform fee | Minimum spend | SMS rate (descending) | MMS rate |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| Starter | $0/mo | $49/mo | $0.015 → $0.009 | $0.045 + carrier |
| Growth | $100/mo | included | $0.01 → $0.008 | $0.03 + carrier |
| Professional | $500/mo | included | $0.007 | $0.024 + carrier |
| Enterprise | Custom | Custom | Custom | Custom |

**2. Starter minimum spend raised from $25 to $49.** A 96% increase in the entry floor. The change pushes pilot brands toward Growth or toward delaying SMS entirely.

**3. Free trial credit reduced from $1,000 to $100.** A 90% reduction in the pilot-evaluation runway. Old trial credit funded ~67K US SMS at the highest rate; new credit funds ~7K SMS. The shift narrows pilots from "test a full holiday campaign" to "test a single broadcast."

**4. Human support replaced by AI bot at lower tiers.** Operator quotes document the loss of named-CSM relationships at Starter/Growth. Professional and Enterprise retain human-team access; below those tiers, support is AI-first.

## Carrier fee passthrough — the third cost layer

Postscript passes carrier fees through with no markup (verified 2026-06-03 against postscript.io/pricing). Typical US rates:

- **US SMS**: $0.0040/msg average carrier fee
- **US MMS**: $0.0083/msg average carrier fee

For a 25,000 US SMS month: carrier passthrough is ~$100. Combined with Growth's $100 platform fee + $250 SMS spend = $450 total. The carrier layer is real and recurring; many operators model only the platform + SMS spend and get surprised by the carrier line item on the invoice.

International SMS runs at $0.25/msg on Starter/Growth/Professional (verified 2026-06-03) — a 25× multiple on US rates. Brands operating internationally should model this separately; the AI plan and Enterprise tier negotiate international rates differently.

## What the pricing model gets wrong

Three things, in order of bill impact:

**1. Onramp reset friction.** Combined effect of $25 → $49 minimum spend and $1,000 → $100 trial credit makes pilot economics meaningfully harder. A 30-day trial that previously funded a full holiday-prep campaign now funds a single broadcast. Postscript's pitch — SMS as a serious revenue channel — requires enough trial volume to demonstrate revenue; the reset narrows that window.

**2. Platform fee on low-send months.** The $100/mo Growth fee and $500/mo Professional fee bill regardless of send volume. Brands with seasonal SMS programs (holiday push + dormant months) pay for the platform during periods when revenue attribution is near zero. The legacy usage-based model didn't have this rhythm. Operators are still adapting their budgeting to the new shape.

**3. AI plan pricing opacity.** The AI Plan (Shopper, Infinity Testing, Conversation Intelligence) is positioned in marketing as a revenue-driving upgrade but the pricing isn't published — it's quote-based. For operators evaluating whether AI plan ROI math works, the lack of public pricing means a sales conversation is required just to model the budget. Adding even a "Starting at $X" hint would reduce evaluation friction.

## Free trial reality check

Postscript's $100 trial credit (verified 2026-06-03) covers approximately 6,500–10,000 US SMS sends depending on volume tier rate, plus carrier passthrough. Practically: enough to test welcome flow, one abandoned-cart automation, and a single broadcast to a small segment (1,000–2,000 subscribers).

What the trial doesn't fund: a full retention-program test across multiple flows, A/B testing of message variants at scale, or a holiday-grade campaign send. For brands needing those validation cycles, plan the trial as "validate setup quality and basic flow logic" — not as a revenue-prove-out.

## When the math doesn't work

Three honest scenarios where Postscript doesn't pencil out:

- **Non-Shopify merchants.** Postscript is Shopify-only. No WooCommerce, no BigCommerce, no Magento. For non-Shopify brands, [Attentive](/tools/attentive) (enterprise SMS), [Klaviyo SMS](/pricing/klaviyo), or [Omnisend's SMS layer](/pricing/omnisend) are the lanes.
- **Brands needing email + SMS + push from one platform.** Postscript is pure-play SMS. For brands wanting bundled multi-channel, [Omnisend Pro](/pricing/omnisend) covers email + SMS + push at $59+/mo with SMS credits = monthly bill. The bundle pencils better at sub-15K subscribers.
- **Pilot stores under $20K MRR.** The $49 minimum + $100 trial credit + carrier passthrough means breakeven SMS revenue is harder to achieve at low MRR. Sub-$20K MRR brands typically run SMS retention through their ESP (Klaviyo/Omnisend SMS) until volume justifies the specialist switch.

## How to lower a Postscript bill without losing capability

In rough order of impact:

1. **Audit your tier monthly against actual send volume.** Brands that grew from Growth into Professional but no longer send the volume that justified it should reconsider. Professional's $500 platform fee only pays back above ~75K SMS/mo.
2. **Move international SMS off Postscript if volume is high.** International rates at $0.25/msg on Starter/Growth/Professional are punishing. International brands at scale should evaluate region-specific platforms or negotiate Enterprise terms.
3. **Reconsider Postscript Plus (managed service) if internal team can handle.** Plus is a done-for-you managed tier — material cost. If your in-house marketing team can run the segmentation and flows, deactivating Plus and self-managing recoups budget.
4. **Use the 4x ROI guarantee as renewal leverage.** If you're not hitting 4x in steady-state, the guarantee triggers refund consideration — which is also leverage at annual renewal for rate negotiation.
5. **Default to 10DLC over dedicated short codes below 100K subscribers.** DSC at $750/mo carrier passthrough is real money; 10DLC throughput covers most brands until ~100K+ subscribers running concurrent campaigns.
