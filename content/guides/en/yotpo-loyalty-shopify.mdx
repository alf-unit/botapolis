[Yotpo](/tools/yotpo) Loyalty & Referrals is one of the more capable loyalty platforms on Shopify in 2026 — and it comes with real context you should understand before committing. This guide covers what it actually does, how to set it up, and the honest caveats, so you can decide whether it fits your store.

## What Yotpo Loyalty actually does

Yotpo Loyalty & Referrals is a no-code loyalty program you launch and adjust without a developer. The core is a points-and-rewards engine with three moving parts:

- **Points** — customers earn points for purchases and for non-purchase actions (account creation, social follows, reviews), then redeem them for store credit at checkout. Yotpo ships 20+ out-of-the-box campaigns like goal-spend bonuses and social-engagement rewards, so you are configuring proven mechanics rather than building from scratch.
- **VIP tiers** — the real retention lever. You group customers into tiers (Bronze / Silver / Gold is the common pattern) based on annual spend or points earned, with better earn rates and exclusive perks at higher tiers. Yotpo supports up to 14 custom tiers plus one automatically generated base tier.
- **Referrals** — every member gets a unique link; when a referred friend makes a purchase, the referrer earns points. It is the cheapest acquisition channel a loyalty program adds, because your existing customers do the marketing.

The strategic advantage of Yotpo specifically is that loyalty does not live in isolation — it shares data with [Yotpo's reviews and UGC products](/best/best-review-app-shopify), so a customer's reviews, purchases, and loyalty status inform one another. That cross-product data is the reason a brand picks Yotpo over a standalone loyalty app.

## How to set it up on Shopify

The five steps in the summary above are the working order. A few practical notes:

1. **Install and connect** from the Shopify App Store — it reads your existing order history, so points can reflect prior spend if you choose to backfill.
2. **Start small on earning rules.** Turn on purchase points plus one or two engagement campaigns. Switching on all 20+ campaigns at launch makes the economics hard to model and confuses customers.
3. **Keep redemption legible.** A shopper should understand what their balance is worth without doing math. Store-credit redemption at checkout is the cleanest model.
4. **Add VIP tiers once you have baseline data.** Two or three tiers tied to annual spend is enough — fourteen is a feature ceiling, not a starting point.
5. **Enable referrals last**, once the earn/redeem loop is working, so referred customers land in a program that already makes sense.

## The honest caveats (read before you commit)

Two things you should weigh:

- **Yotpo sunset its SMS and Email products on December 31, 2025**, alongside a roughly 34% staff cut. Loyalty and reviews are now the company's focus, which is fine — but if you were hoping to consolidate email or SMS into the same vendor, that path is closed. Plan your retention stack around Yotpo for loyalty and reviews, not as an all-in-one.
- **Pricing is enterprise-shaped.** There is a free tier for early-stage brands (basic points, a rewards widget, and referral tools at no cost), which is a genuine way to start. But the paid plans — Loyalty Pro around $199/mo and Premium around $799/mo — land Yotpo's bundled cost in the $12K–$30K/yr range in practice. See the [Yotpo pricing breakdown](/pricing/yotpo) before you scope a paid tier.

## Is Yotpo Loyalty right for your store?

Pick Yotpo Loyalty when you want a sophisticated, customizable program — VIP tiers, 20+ campaigns, referrals — and especially when you also run (or plan to run) Yotpo reviews, so the two share data. For a smaller store that just wants a simple points program live in days, [Smile.io](/tools/smile-io) is the easier, cheaper starting point — we compare the loyalty options in the [best loyalty app roundup](/best/best-loyalty-app-shopify). For a brand already invested in Yotpo's ecosystem, the loyalty product is a strong, retention-focused choice.
