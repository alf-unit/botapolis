## Why this list is two tools, and a disclosure

Upsell and cross-sell on Shopify is a narrow, serious category in 2026, and our catalogue has two tools worth ranking: [Rebuy](/tools/rebuy) and [LimeSpot](/tools/limespot). We are not padding it with generic "boost your AOV" widgets or apps that bolt a single post-purchase offer onto checkout and call it a personalization platform. These two are real recommendation engines, both Built-for-Shopify, both rated 8.9/10 in our catalogue.

One disclosure up front: **neither Rebuy nor LimeSpot runs an affiliate program we earn from.** That makes this a purely editorial ranking — the order below tracks fit, not commissions, because there are no commissions. Prices verified directly on each vendor's pricing page (LimeSpot 2026-06-04, Rebuy 2026-05-30).

## #1 — Rebuy (the pick for cart and checkout personalization)

[Rebuy](/tools/rebuy) is the stronger pick when your upsell strategy lives in the cart and at checkout. Its Smart Cart drives measurable AOV lifts through cross-sell and upsell widgets across the cart, checkout extensions, and post-purchase pages, with 35+ integrations and no-code/low-code building blocks. For Shopify and Shopify Plus stores with a diverse catalogue (10+ SKUs), that breadth is the difference between a single static upsell and a personalization layer that adapts per shopper.

Pricing is tiered and climbs quickly: a Build-Your-Plan option from $25/mo, Starter at $99/mo, Grow at $749/mo, and Enterprise at $999/mo+. Two honest caveats: initial widget setup can be complex (this is a platform, not a one-click app), and the Grow tier at $749/mo is steep for small brands. For stores where a few points of AOV lift clears that cost, Rebuy pays back; below that, the price tag outruns the return. See the [Rebuy pricing breakdown](/pricing/rebuy) for the tier math.

## #2 — LimeSpot (the pick for AI product recommendations)

[LimeSpot](/tools/limespot) (8.9/10) is the choice when AI-driven product recommendations are the actual job — personalized recommendation carousels, cross-sell and upsell blocks across the storefront, set up without a developer. Where Rebuy centres on the cart, LimeSpot spreads personalization across the whole shopping journey (home, collection, product, cart). Support is a repeated strength in reviews, and theme installation on OS 2.0 is genuinely easy.

Pricing is revenue-banded: LimeSpot Max is $150/mo for up to $50,000 in 30-day online-store revenue (a 50% lifetime discount promo for new signups makes that effectively ~$75/mo), with higher revenue bands costing more. The honest caveat: because pricing is tied to store revenue, it escalates as you grow, and a couple of reviews flag attribution questions (draft products surfacing in recommendations). For a brand that wants storefront-wide AI personalization without engineering time, it earns the second slot. The [LimeSpot pricing breakdown](/pricing/limespot) covers the revenue bands.

## The honest verdict

Pick **Rebuy** when your AOV lever is the cart and checkout — Smart Cart, post-purchase offers, and deep cart personalization are its core, and the integration breadth suits a complex catalogue. Pick **LimeSpot** when storefront-wide AI product recommendations are the job and you want them live without a developer, accepting that revenue-banded pricing climbs as you scale.

We do not list a third upsell app, because the catalogue does not have a third we would put in front of an operator in mid-2026 — and we tell you plainly that neither of these two pays us, so the ranking is fit and nothing else. If your real need is broader merchandising, the [Rebuy vs cart-personalization](/tools/rebuy) and recommendation comparisons are the next read; for pure post-purchase retention, returns and subscriptions are different jobs covered elsewhere on the site.
