Pencil's pricing page opens at $14/mo, which makes it look like a cheap AI ad tool. It isn't. Since the Brandtech acquisition in 2023, Pencil has been built as an enterprise AI creative operating system — governance, multi-model orchestration, brand-safety controls, named customers like Diageo, L'Oréal, and Unilever — and the $14 Core and $55 Growth tiers are essentially a self-serve trial of a platform designed for global brands and large agencies. The real product is the Pro tier, and that's a sales quote.

This page does the real math: every tier, what the generation caps and credit burn actually mean, what's gated to Pro, and where AdCreative.ai is the better SMB call. Prices verified 2026-06-04 from trypencil.com/pricing.

## Bottom line up front

- **Best for:** global enterprise brands and large agencies needing governance, multi-model AI creative, and multi-market scale.
- **Avoid if:** you're a small Shopify merchant or solo founder wanting one-click ad generation — AdCreative.ai fits better at SMB prices.
- **Standout strength:** Pencil Score's predictive accuracy (published 84% / 91% on ~$2B of tracked ad spend) plus the Open Garden multi-model orchestration.
- **Biggest weakness:** the self-serve tiers are a trial; the real governance-and-agents product is at Pro, which is quote-only.

## The tiers in numbers (verified 2026-06-04)

| Plan | Monthly | Annual (20% off) | Generations | What unlocks |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Core | $14/mo | $11/mo | 50/mo | Self-serve AI agents, Pencil Score, multi-platform launch, 1 workspace, chat support |
| Growth | $55/mo | $44/mo | 250/mo | + unlimited workspaces, direct product-team access |
| Pro | Custom | Custom | Unlimited | + bulk generation via feeds, role-based access, enterprise terms, AI governance, dedicated success |

A free trial gives you 6 ads on signup; there's no permanent free tier. The structural fact: the self-serve tiers (Core, Growth) are capped and individual; the enterprise capability — governance, agents, bulk feeds, managed service — lives behind the Pro quote.

## What a "generation" costs, and why the cap is tricky

A generation is one AI creative output, and self-serve plans are capped monthly: 50 on Core, 250 on Growth. The wrinkle is that credit burn varies by the AI model you invoke — heavier models like Google Veo 3 consume more per generation than a standard image model. So Core's 50-generation cap isn't a flat 50 deliverables; a video-heavy month burns through it faster than a static-image month.

For a team experimenting across Pencil's Open Garden model set (OpenAI, Google Gemini/Veo, Adobe Firefly, Bria, Runway), this makes Core's quota unpredictable. Exceeding it means upgrading, not paying per-overage. If your usage is steady and image-led, Core or Growth is workable; if it's video-heavy or exploratory, model your generation budget before committing.

## What Pro buys that self-serve doesn't

The self-serve tiers are creative generation with a score attached. Pro is the platform Pencil actually markets:

- **Unlimited generations** with committed-consumption pricing.
- **Bulk generation via feeds** — generate translation, personalization, and creative variants programmatically.
- **Role-based access controls** and multi-stakeholder review workflows with audit trails.
- **Enterprise terms** — IP assignment, no-train data policy, indemnification, data ringfencing.
- **AI governance** plus a training curriculum and a dedicated success manager.
- **Managed service** — a shared or dedicated creative strategist at the Pro and Enterprise levels.

This is the procurement-grade layer that justifies Pencil for a regulated global brand. None of it is in Core or Growth.

## Is the predictive scoring worth it?

Pencil's headline claim is the Pencil Score: published accuracy of 84% for winning ads and 91% for losing ads, measured against roughly $2B of tracked ad spend across 4,000+ brands. For a procurement team evaluating whether to trust pre-launch scoring, those are credible, act-on-able numbers — but only at the volume where predictive scoring beats the cost of human creative QA. The honest operator caveat on output quality:

> AI gets you 90% of the way to a finished product. As you input 10 creatives, it can often push out a video that is not quite ad ready.
>

> Pencil allows us the opportunity to quickly turn one creative into so many more.

## When the math works — and when it doesn't

**It pays back when:**
- You're a global enterprise brand or large agency needing governance, brand-safety, and multi-market scale.
- You have a dedicated creative or marketing-ops team to run the platform and finish the last 10%.
- Your ad volume is high enough that predictive scoring saves more than human QA would cost.

**It doesn't pay back when:**
- You're a small Shopify merchant or solo founder — AdCreative.ai is the right-sized, one-click, SMB-priced fit.
- You have no dedicated creative team to operate a governance-heavy platform.
- Your usage is low — the per-creative value of enterprise scoring doesn't clear the overhead.
