## What changed in 2026 (and why old comparisons are wrong)

Most "Klaviyo vs Mailchimp" articles still ranking in 2026 were written in 2021–2024, and the comparison they describe is no longer accurate. Four shifts invalidate the old verdict:

**October 26, 2021 — Mailchimp returns to the Shopify App Store.** Mailchimp acquired ShopSync and used it to ship a new native Shopify integration after the 2019 public breakup. Per Modern Retail's coverage of the announcement: *"Mailchimp is launching an integration for Shopify more than two years after a public bust-up between the two companies… Mailchimp is announcing that it has acquired one of those integrations, ShopSync, to power its new app."* The connector is now maintained by Intuit Mailchimp at apps.shopify.com/mailchimp.

**February 18, 2025 — Klaviyo shifts to active-profile billing.** Klaviyo replaced its old "total contacts" model with active-profile pricing. For stores with engaged audiences this is neutral or favorable; for stores carrying years of inactive contacts, bills jumped 30–60% overnight.

**June 2025 — Mailchimp deprecates the Classic Automation Builder.** Anything more complex than a single-step "if X then send Y" automation now requires the Customer Journey Builder, which is locked behind the Standard plan. Essentials users lost the ability to run multi-step abandonment, post-purchase, or welcome series at the same price they were paying.

**January 2026 — Mailchimp cuts the free tier.** The old free tier (500 contacts, 1,000 sends) became 250 contacts, 500 sends, with a daily cap of 250. Mailchimp and Klaviyo free tiers are now functionally identical on volume, which removes one of Mailchimp's longest-standing advantages.

The net: comparisons written before mid-2025 systematically overstate Mailchimp's value at the entry tier and understate Klaviyo's integration with Shopify.

## Feature comparison

### Shopify integration

Mailchimp's connector is now official and maintained, but the sync model is batch, not real-time. Product catalog and order events typically land 5–15 minutes after they happen. Klaviyo's connector pushes events within ~30 seconds and exposes the full event stream (`Placed Order`, `Started Checkout`, `Viewed Product`) as first-class profile data.

For a store running broadcast campaigns once a week, batch sync is invisible. For abandonment flows that trigger on `Started Checkout`, the lag matters — a 15-minute delay between abandonment and first email cuts recovery rates measurably.

**Winner: Klaviyo,** on sync speed and event model depth.

### Automations

This is where the 2025 Mailchimp downgrade bites. Mailchimp Essentials can work for newsletter-style campaigns and single-step automation, but multi-step ecommerce journeys require Standard after the June 2025 Classic Automation Builder deprecation. If you wanted a flow like *email 1 at 1 hour → SMS at 24 hours → email 2 at 48 hours with a different offer*, you used to build it in Essentials. After June 2025, it lives in Standard only.

Klaviyo includes ecommerce segmentation, predictive analytics, and automation templates on the paid Email plan; pricing scales by active profile count rather than feature tier. Klaviyo's library ships with 80+ pre-built ecom flow templates that work the moment the Shopify connector is live.

**Winner: Klaviyo,** unless you only need single-step automations.

### Segmentation

Mailchimp's segmentation engine handles basic conditions well: tag-based, purchase history, engagement scores. What it can't do natively (and Klaviyo can): segment on viewed-product-but-didn't-buy windows, predicted churn risk, predicted lifetime value, or refunds reversed back into the profile.

For a Shopify store building VIP segments or running a win-back motion, those four are the segments you actually want. Klaviyo's predictive analytics features ship on the paid Email plan without an upcharge.

**Winner: Klaviyo,** decisively.

### Reporting

Mailchimp's reporting is cleaner and more visually digestible — particularly the comparative campaign view that overlays multiple sends. Klaviyo's reporting is denser, more detailed, and harder to scan but exposes flow-level revenue attribution, which Mailchimp doesn't surface natively.

For an operator trying to answer *"which automation made me money last month,"* Klaviyo answers directly. For an operator trying to answer *"how did this campaign compare to my last three,"* Mailchimp is faster to read.

**Winner: Tie.** Different mental models, both valid.

### Support

Software Advice ratings as of February 23, 2026: Klaviyo 4.6/5 from 521 verified reviews; Mailchimp 4.5/5 from 17,535 verified reviews. The volume gap reflects Mailchimp's deeper non-ecom user base. Klaviyo's support skews toward the ecom use case directly — agents understand Shopify flows out of the box.

**Winner: Slight Klaviyo,** especially for Shopify-specific tickets.

## Migration & switching cost

**Mailchimp → Klaviyo.** Export your subscribers as CSV from Mailchimp (Audience → Manage Audience → Export). Klaviyo accepts that CSV directly via List Import, but you'll lose: engagement scores, automation logic (rebuild from scratch), email template styling (Mailchimp's drag-and-drop blocks don't map 1:1 to Klaviyo's universal blocks), and consent history beyond opt-in date. Plan a 1-2 week deliverability ramp on Klaviyo's shared IPs.

Realistic timeline: a 2,000-contact store with three automations migrates cleanly in 8–12 hours of focused work. Most of that is rebuilding automation logic in Klaviyo's flow builder, not the data move.

**Klaviyo → Mailchimp.** The same export-as-CSV move works in reverse, but you lose more on the way back: predictive analytics fields, flow-level revenue attribution history, and the entire active-profile billing model. If you're moving back to Mailchimp specifically to save on bills, the math only works below ~750 active profiles in 2026.

For both directions: keep both accounts active for 30 days post-cutover so you have a fallback if deliverability drops on the new platform.

## Our recommendation

### Choose Mailchimp if:

- You're under 1,000 contacts and only need newsletters + a single abandonment email.
- Your team includes non-marketers who'll touch the platform — Mailchimp's learning curve is genuinely lower.
- You're paying $13–$20/mo and you want to keep paying $13–$20/mo without thinking about it.

### Choose Klaviyo if:

- You want multi-step automation flows (welcome series, abandonment with SMS, post-purchase product recommendations).
- Your segmentation needs include predicted CLV, churn risk, or browse-but-didn't-buy windows.
- You're planning to scale past 1,500 contacts within 6–12 months and want to skip the migration.

### Tie-breaker

The decisive question is *do you need multi-step automation logic?* If yes, Mailchimp pushes you to Standard, which costs the same as Klaviyo and does less on the ecommerce side. If no, Mailchimp Essentials at $13/mo at 500 contacts is the cheapest legitimate option in the category.

Most Shopify operators discover the multi-step need within the first 90 days of running serious email. Plan around that, not around launch-day economics.
