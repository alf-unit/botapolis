Judge.me's pricing is almost too simple to need an explainer: it's free, or it's $15/mo. That's it. The $15 Awesome tier is flat — the most you can ever pay, no matter how many orders you process or reviews you collect — which makes Judge.me the predictable-cost default for Shopify reviews while most competitors scale their bills with your order volume.

This page covers what the free plan actually includes, what the $15 tier adds, and where Loox or Yotpo is worth the premium instead. Verified 2026-06-04 from judge.me/pricing.

## Bottom line up front

- **Best for:** Shopify merchants of every size who want unlimited reviews on a free or near-free plan.
- **Avoid if:** you need high-polish visual review feeds (Loox) or an enterprise UGC-and-loyalty suite (Yotpo).
- **Standout strength:** the flat $15/mo ceiling — predictable cost regardless of order volume.
- **Biggest weakness:** widget design is less polished than Loox, and the AI support bot frustrates before you reach a human.

## The two plans in numbers (verified 2026-06-04)

| Plan | Monthly | What you get |
|---|---|---|
| Forever Free | $0 | Unlimited reviews and requests, photo/video reviews, in-email forms, SEO rich snippets, carousel widgets — with Judge.me branding |
| Awesome | $15/mo | Everything in Free, no branding, plus AI summaries, AI reply drafts, Q&A, site reviews, referrals/coupons, Mailchimp sync, Google Shopping syndication |

There's a 15-day free trial of Awesome. The structural fact that defines Judge.me: **the price doesn't scale**. $15 is the ceiling whether you do 100 orders a month or 100,000.

## The free plan is the real story

Most "free" reviews plans are demos with hard caps. Judge.me's Forever Free is a working product: unlimited review request emails, unlimited reviews displayed, unlimited orders, photo and video reviews, and — the part that matters for SEO — automatic Review schema rich snippets injected into your product pages. A lot of stores run on the free plan indefinitely and never feel the need to upgrade.

What you give up on Free: Judge.me branding sits on your widgets and emails, and the marketing-and-polish features are gated to Awesome. For a store validating whether reviews move conversion, Free is more than enough to get a real answer before spending a cent.

## What the $15 Awesome tier adds

Awesome ($15/mo) is the marketing layer:

- **No branding** — remove the Judge.me mark from widgets and emails.
- **AI Review Summaries** — condense many reviews into a shoppable summary block.
- **AI reply drafts** — AI-generated reply suggestions to customer reviews.
- **Q&A, site reviews, custom forms** — store-level reviews and structured Q&A.
- **Referrals and coupons** — a built-in referral program.
- **Google Shopping syndication** — Judge.me is an official Google partner, so your ratings flow into Google Shopping ads and free listings.

For a store running paid Google Shopping or wanting branded widgets, $15/mo pays for itself quickly. For a store that just needs reviews collected and displayed, Free already covers it.

## The flat-rate advantage

This is where Judge.me's pricing model actually wins money. Reviews apps that bill on order volume — [Loox](/tools/loox), [Yotpo](/tools/yotpo) — get more expensive as you grow. Judge.me's $15 ceiling means a store doing 50,000 orders a month pays the same $15 as one doing 500. For high-volume stores, that gap is real: a usage-priced app can run $50–$300+/mo at scale, against Judge.me's flat $15.

## When the math works — and when it doesn't

**It pays back when:**
- You want unlimited reviews on a genuinely free or $15-flat plan.
- You're high-volume and usage-priced apps would cost you far more.
- You need SEO rich snippets and basic review collection without polish overhead.

**It doesn't pay back when:**
- You need high-polish, curated visual review feeds — Loox's widget design is the reason to pay more.
- You want reviews, loyalty, SMS, and UGC marketing consolidated in one enterprise vendor — Yotpo fits better.
- Widget customization without code is a priority — Judge.me's themes need more manual work.

## Alternatives worth considering

- **[Loox](/tools/loox)** — the visual-first premium: better-looking photo and video widgets, a conversion suite at the Convert tier. Worth the higher price when visual proof drives your conversion.
- **[Yotpo](/tools/yotpo)** — the all-in-one for enterprise brands wanting reviews plus loyalty plus SMS in one vendor.
