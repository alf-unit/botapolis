LoyaltyLion sits in the mid-market loyalty band, and its pricing reflects that positioning in a way that catches people off guard: only one tier shows a price. The $199/mo Classic plan is public; Advanced and Plus are quote-only, scaled on order volume through a sales conversation. That's not a gotcha so much as a signal — LoyaltyLion is built for brands that want a customized, on-brand loyalty program and have the order volume to justify a sales-led purchase, not merchants shopping for a $20/mo rewards widget.

This page covers what's actually knowable: the Classic price, the order-volume caps that determine your tier, the upsell trigger, what unlocks where, and where Smile.io is the better-value call. Verified 2026-06-04 from loyaltylion.com/pricing.

## Bottom line up front

- **Best for:** mid-market and Shopify Plus DTC brands and omnichannel retailers wanting a customized loyalty experience wired into Klaviyo.
- **Avoid if:** you're early-stage or want a cheap pop-up rewards widget — Smile.io wins on price and launch speed.
- **Standout strength:** depth of customization plus genuine Klaviyo/Attentive event integration (LoyaltyLion is a Klaviyo Elite Master Partner).
- **Biggest weakness:** opaque pricing past Classic, enforced order caps, and an upsell trigger that steps your cost up with order growth.

## The tiers in numbers (verified 2026-06-04)

| Plan | Monthly | Order cap (existing) | What unlocks |
|---|---|---|---|
| Free (Shopify App) | $0 | Low | Feature-limited starter |
| Classic | $199/mo | ~2,000 | Branded loyalty page, earning rules, Klaviyo integration, onboarding |
| Advanced | Custom (quote) | ~4,000 | + VIP tiers, points on product pages, enhanced POS, multilingual, Klaviyo/Attentive events |
| Plus | Custom (quote) | 10,000+ | + AI-recommended rewards, advanced Klaviyo events, LoyaltyLion Flows, in-cart rewards, quarterly reviews |

The thing to internalize: pricing tracks **order volume**, not revenue. A high-AOV brand with modest order counts can sit on Classic while doing strong revenue; a low-AOV, high-volume brand hits the caps faster and gets pushed up.

## The order-cap mechanic and the upsell trigger

Each tier carries a monthly order cap. For existing customers the figures run roughly: Small Business 800, Classic 2,000, Advanced 4,000, Plus 10,000+. The mechanic that catches growing brands: if your monthly orders exceed the contracted threshold by more than 25% for three consecutive months, LoyaltyLion reaches out to move you up a tier.

This isn't hidden or punitive — it's how usage-based loyalty pricing works — but it means your loyalty cost is a step function tied to order growth. Budget for the upsell cycle if you're scaling, and don't anchor on the $199 Classic price if your order volume is heading toward the Advanced band.

## What you get at each tier

**Classic ($199/mo)** is the on-brand foundation: a branded loyalty page (LoyaltyLion will build it from a template), pre-built earning and redemption rules, the Klaviyo integration, and dedicated onboarding. For a brand that wants loyalty to look like part of the store rather than a third-party pop-up, Classic is the real entry — and the customization is the reason to be here over a templated widget.

**Advanced (quote)** adds the retention-driving mechanics:

- **VIP tiers** with escalating benefits.
- **Points on product pages** — surfacing earnable points pre-purchase.
- **Enhanced Shopify POS** for omnichannel earn/redeem.
- **Multilingual** support (130+ languages) for international brands.
- **Klaviyo and Attentive loyalty events** — real-time triggers (points, tier changes, rewards) into your email and SMS flows. This is the integration depth that justifies LoyaltyLion for brands already invested in [Klaviyo](/tools/klaviyo).

**Plus (quote)** is the enterprise layer: AI-recommended rewards and targeted discounts, advanced Klaviyo events, automated LoyaltyLion Flows with conditional logic, in-cart rewards and instant points, cross-store rewards for multi-brand portfolios, and quarterly strategic reviews with dedicated loyalty experts.

LoyaltyLion's customer-success reputation is genuinely strong — reviewers name individual specialists, and the relationship-led onboarding is a recurring positive:

But the support experience isn't uniform, and the negative reports are specific:

The reconciliation: customer success (onboarding, strategy) earns praise; reactive technical support on critical issues is where the slow-resolution complaints cluster. The Trustpilot 5.0 from 53 reviews reflects post-onboarding solicited feedback, so weight the broader G2 (4.6 from 500) and Shopify App Store (4.6 from 609) as the load-bearing third-party signal.

## When the math works — and when it doesn't

**It pays back when:**
- You're mid-market or Shopify Plus and want loyalty that looks and behaves on-brand, not like a bolt-on widget.
- You're already running Klaviyo or Attentive and want loyalty events feeding those flows.
- You have the order volume where retained repeat-purchase revenue clears the $199+/mo cost.

**It doesn't pay back when:**
- You're early-stage and need to validate whether loyalty moves your repeat rate at all — start cheaper.
- You want a free or low-cost rewards widget live today — that's Smile.io's zone.
- Your order volume is low and high-AOV — you'll pay for tier capacity you don't use.
