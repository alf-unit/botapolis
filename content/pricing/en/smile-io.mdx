Smile.io's pitch is that loyalty shouldn't need a developer, and on that promise it delivers — a points program and referrals live the same afternoon, a Built-for-Shopify badge, native POS earn/redeem, and a free tier that genuinely works. The pricing question isn't whether the free plan is real (it is, under 200 monthly orders). It's what happens after that, because the paid curve moved in 2026: the entry paid tier is now $79/mo, not the $49 it used to be, and the features that make loyalty actually compound — VIP tiers, the Loyalty Hub — sit at $199/mo Growth.

This page does the real math: every tier, what unlocks where, and the order-count quirk that pushes stores up the pricing ladder faster than their revenue does. Prices verified 2026-06-04 from smile.io's own pricing page.

## Bottom line up front

- **Best for:** small-to-mid Shopify merchants and Shopify Plus brands in repeat-purchase categories (beauty, supplements, fashion) that want a branded loyalty program live in days.
- **Avoid if:** you need highly customized program logic, advanced gamification, or deep enterprise CRM integration — that's LoyaltyLion or Yotpo Loyalty territory.
- **Standout strength:** the easiest loyalty setup on Shopify, full stop, plus a support reputation strong enough that reviewers name individual reps.
- **Biggest weakness:** the order-count denominator. It includes refunds and unpaid orders, so your billing tier tracks gross order events, not paid revenue.

And the entry paid tier repriced upward in 2026 (the old $49 Starter at 500 orders became $79 Standard at 1,000 orders) — more order headroom, but a higher floor.

## The order-count trap

This is the quiet driver of bigger Smile.io bills, and it's stated plainly on the pricing page:

> Every order placed at your store during that month contributes to your monthly order total. This includes all orders, whether they are paid, unpaid, or refunded.

Read that twice if you run a category with returns. A fashion store with a 25-30% return rate is paying for loyalty tier capacity on orders that got refunded — the order event counts even when the revenue reverses. Same with abandoned/unpaid orders that Shopify records. The practical effects:

- A store doing 1,800 paid orders but 2,400 gross order events (refunds + unpaid included) is a Growth-tier store, not a Standard one, regardless of revenue.
- A seasonal spike (BFCM, a viral moment) can push you into overage — $20 per additional 100 orders on Standard, $5 per 100 on Growth — or trigger an auto-upgrade.
- The cheapest lever to manage this isn't on Smile.io's side; it's keeping your refund and cancellation hygiene tight in Shopify, because that's the denominator.

## What you actually get at each tier

**Free (under 200 orders)** is a working loyalty program, not a demo. Points, referrals, same-day launch. For a store validating whether loyalty moves repeat rate, this is enough to get a real signal before spending a dollar. The cap is the 200-order ceiling plus no VIP tiers and limited branding control.

**Standard ($79/mo, 1,000 orders)** adds the polish: a dedicated, SEO-friendly loyalty page, product-page embeds, 2x points promotional events, two integrations, and 20 reports. This is the tier for a store that's confirmed loyalty works and wants it branded and visible — but it's still pre-VIP.

**Growth ($199/mo, 2,500 orders)** is where loyalty starts to compound, and it's the tier most serious programs actually need:

- **VIP tiers** (Bronze/Silver/Gold) with escalating benefits — the single biggest driver of repeat-purchase behavior in a points program.
- **Loyalty Hub** inside Shopify customer accounts — a native rewards home, not a bolt-on widget.
- **Redeem at checkout** — points redemption in the checkout flow, which lifts redemption rates.
- **Unlimited integrations** — including deep [Klaviyo](/tools/klaviyo) triggers off points balance, tier changes, and referral activity. This is the retention-email multiplier: segment your Klaviyo flows by loyalty tier and the open/conversion lift is real.
- Performance benchmarks and 25 reports.

**Plus ($999/mo, annual only, 7,500 orders)** adds API access (the integration ceiling), a migration/launch plan, quarterly program monitoring, priority support, and 30+ reports, with $5-per-100 overage above 7,500 orders. It's credible at scale but a sharp cliff for a mid-market store sitting at 3,000-5,000 orders that needs the API but not the rest.

## The Klaviyo integration is the real value at Growth

The reason Growth is worth the step from Standard isn't just VIP tiers — it's that unlimited integrations unlock the deep Klaviyo connection. You can trigger Klaviyo flows off a customer's points balance, a tier change, or referral activity, which means your retention emails segment by loyalty status. A "you're 50 points from Gold" flow converts at a different rate than a generic newsletter. For stores already running Klaviyo (most serious Shopify brands), this is where loyalty stops being a standalone widget and starts feeding the retention engine. ## When the math works — and when it doesn't

**It pays back when:**
- You're in a repeat-purchase category where loyalty moves the repeat rate (beauty, supplements, fashion, consumables).
- You're already on Klaviyo and can wire loyalty tier into retention flows at Growth.
- You want it live in days, not a quarter of engineering.

**It doesn't pay back when:**
- Your AOV is high and purchase frequency is low (furniture, one-time big-ticket) — points programs don't move infrequent buyers much.
- You need complex, custom rules logic or advanced gamification — you'll outgrow Smile.io's intentional simplicity.
- You're past $1M ARR and need enterprise segmentation and dedicated account management — see the alternatives below.
