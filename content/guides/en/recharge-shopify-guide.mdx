[Recharge](/tools/recharge) is the incumbent subscription platform on Shopify — by its own claim, it powers 71% of subscriptions sold on Shopify stores. This guide covers its features, its integration, and the trade-offs that come with the incumbent.

## Native Shopify integration

Recharge runs subscriptions inside Shopify's native checkout (no iframes), via the Recharge–Shopify Checkout Integration. Its real advantage is ecosystem depth: as the category incumbent, it has the broadest set of partner integrations (Klaviyo, Gorgias, and the rest of the stack you probably already run), which is the reason large catalogues and complex tooling stacks stay on it.

## The features that matter

- **Concierge SMS** — an AI feature (Plus plan) that lets subscribers skip, delay, swap, or add products by text in natural language.
- **Bundles** — curated and customizable subscription bundles with tiered discounts.
- **Rewards & referrals** and **win-back / retention campaigns** to extend subscriber lifetime.
- **Cart and post-purchase upsell** — convert one-time orders into subscriptions inside the cart.

## The honest caveats

Recharge is battle-tested but shows its age. Honest trade-offs: it is built on older code that causes intermittent issues, out-of-box checkout customization is limited, and the Plus tier requires a 12-month contract. Pricing (verified 2026-06-05): public Starter $99/mo + 1.49% + 19¢ per transaction (month-to-month), Plus $499/mo + 1.34% + 19¢ (12-month term). A constrained $25/mo new-merchant tier exists off the public page but auto-bumps to $99 at 50 subscribers — treat $99 as the real floor. Recharge also acquired Skio for $105M in April 2026, so expect roadmap and pricing moves over the next 12–24 months. See the [Recharge pricing breakdown](/pricing/recharge).
