> **TL;DR.** For Shopify stores under 25,000 contacts in 2026, the answer splits on what you're optimizing. **Pick Omnisend** if you want cheaper math, faster setup, and the new MCP/ChatGPT integration — typical sub-5k stores save 30–50% per month vs Klaviyo. **Pick Klaviyo** if you need ecommerce flow depth (browse abandonment, post-purchase product recommendations, predicted CLV), or if you're already paying for support and want Customer Agent's email-attributed conversation handling. The April 1, 2026 Customer Agent jump from $140 to $200 doesn't change this — it's a separate $200/mo add-on, not a baseline price bump.

## Pricing showdown

Both vendors moved their pricing in 2026, and the differences matter more than the headline numbers.

| Plan | 500 contacts | 1,500 contacts | 5,000 contacts | 15,000 contacts | 25,000 contacts |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Free | $0 (250 cap) | n/a | n/a | n/a | n/a |
| Email | $20 | ~$45 | ~$100 | ~$245 | ~$385 |
| Email + SMS (+$15 base) | $35 | ~$60 | ~$115 | ~$260 | ~$400 |

Klaviyo pricing notes:
- **Feature parity across every paid tier** — you don't unlock new features by paying more; price scales only with active profile count.
- **February 18, 2025: active-profiles billing.** Klaviyo shifted from billing on "profiles you email" to "all active profiles" (anyone active in roughly the last 365 days). Dormant subscribers count.
- **April 1, 2026: Customer Agent regular pricing.** Introductory $140/mo replaced by $200/mo regular rate. Separate from Email plan — can be skipped entirely.
- **Klaviyo One mandatory above $10,000/mo standard spend** — adds 20% to the bill. Doesn't apply at sub-25k contacts unless heavy SMS usage.

Omnisend pricing notes:

- **SMS bundled on Pro** vs Klaviyo's per-message overage model. For high-SMS senders, Omnisend Pro can pencil out cheaper than Klaviyo Email + SMS at the same band.
- **MCP integration** — Omnisend's 2026 differentiator. Lets ChatGPT and other compatible AI clients query the Omnisend account conversationally. No separate cost.
- No equivalent of Klaviyo One's surcharge at higher tiers.

### Real-world cost delta at 5,000 contacts

A typical sub-15k Shopify store carries ~30% dormant-but-active-by-Klaviyo's-definition profiles (no sunset flow, list growth from acquisitions a year+ ago that never re-engaged). So a 5,000-subscribed-contacts store on Omnisend is closer to 6,500 active profiles on Klaviyo. That widens the apparent delta:

- **Omnisend Standard at 5,000 subscribed:** ~$51/mo
- **Klaviyo Email at ~6,500 active profiles:** ~$120/mo

Net: roughly 2.4× more on Klaviyo for the same actual subscriber base, before any Customer Agent or Klaviyo One add-ons. Aggressive list hygiene on Klaviyo (sunset flows, suppression sweeps) closes that gap to ~$100/mo — still about 2× Omnisend.

## The 2026 AI feature delta

This is the part most "Klaviyo vs Omnisend" comparisons still get wrong by treating it as a vendor-equivalence issue. The two products solve different problems.

**Omnisend MCP (Model Context Protocol).** Operator-facing. Connect ChatGPT (or any MCP-compatible AI client) to your Omnisend account, then ask in natural language: "show me my best-performing campaign last quarter and suggest a follow-up to the 10% of subscribers who clicked but didn't buy". The AI queries the live data. Use cases: campaign post-mortems, segment ideation, performance debugging without clicking through five reports. Verify the actual current scope on omnisend.com/features 2026-05-26 — what MCP exposes evolves as Omnisend ships new endpoints.

**Klaviyo Customer Agent.** Shopper-facing. Handles conversational interactions with your customers — order status queries, FAQ answers, return initiation. Routes to a human on complex disputes or complaints. The closest analog isn't another email tool's AI — it's Gorgias or Intercom's chatbot tier. Price: $200/mo regular (from April 1, 2026), separate from the Email plan.

A 5,000-contact store running both products would pay ~$51/mo Omnisend Standard (with MCP included) + nothing else for AI; on Klaviyo, ~$100/mo Email plus +$200/mo Customer Agent = $300/mo total. Different value propositions, very different price points.

**Which one matters for you depends on whether you're solving an analytics problem or a support-volume problem.** They're not substitutes.

## Feature comparison

**Automations and flows.** Klaviyo's depth is in the 80+ pre-built ecommerce flow templates — welcome, abandoned cart, browse abandonment, post-purchase, win-back, price drop, back-in-stock, replenishment — with conditional splits, A/B within flows, and dynamic wait times. Omnisend covers the headline flows (welcome, cart, browse, post-purchase) on Standard but the conditional logic is shallower; multi-branch decision trees that Klaviyo handles natively often require Omnisend Pro plus manual setup.

**Segmentation.** Klaviyo: unlimited conditions per segment on Email plan; predicted CLV, churn risk, next purchase date, behavioral filters. Omnisend: solid demographic + engagement + purchase-history segmentation on Standard; predictive segmentation is lighter than Klaviyo's. The practical test: can you build a segment like "viewed product in collection X in the last 14 days, predicted CLV >$200, hasn't purchased in 60 days"? Klaviyo yes. Omnisend, partially — the predictive CLV piece is the gap.

**Shopify integration.** Both are real-time-native. Klaviyo's edge is event granularity — every `Placed Order`, `Started Checkout`, `Viewed Product` populates as a discrete profile event with full payload, which is what enables the rich segmentation above. Omnisend's integration syncs customers, products, and orders cleanly but with lighter event metadata. For broadcast-newsletter use cases, neither edge matters. For behavioral flows, Klaviyo's depth shows up.

**Deliverability.** Both adequate at sub-25k volume. Klaviyo recommends dedicated sending-domain setup past 5,000 active profiles; Omnisend handles deliverability on shared IPs through 50,000 contacts with no friction reports in 2026 operator threads. SPF/DKIM/DMARC setup is comparable.

**SMS.** Omnisend Pro bundles SMS credits into the monthly plan; Klaviyo charges per-message overage on top of the +$15 SMS plan add-on. For stores doing meaningful SMS volume (>1,000 sends/mo), Omnisend Pro can be the cheaper option.

**Support.** Klaviyo: email and chat support, with reported queue times of 6–24 hours on the lower tiers. Omnisend: 24/7 email and chat across all paid tiers, including Standard. Premium support tier on Klaviyo One only.

## Where the comparison gets weak

Two things this article cannot honestly verify:

1. **MCP operator outcomes are still thin.** The MCP integration shipped in 2026 and verifiable operator case studies at scale don't yet exist on r/shopify, G2, or Reddit. We've described the integration's scope per Omnisend's own docs — the actual "MCP saved us 4 hours/week on reporting" type claims aren't verified anywhere we trust.
2. **Customer Agent operator outcomes are vendor-sourced.** Klaviyo's Customer Agent product is similarly new (rolled out into 2026 alongside K:AI Marketing Agent). Independent operator reports on the $200/mo price point's actual L1-deflection rate aren't established. Anyone publishing specific "Customer Agent saved us $X/mo" numbers in May 2026 is citing vendor case studies or speculating.

If your decision hinges on either AI feature's real-world ROI, ask both vendors for current customer references in your contact-size band before committing.

## Migration cost (either direction)

What moves cleanly:
- Contact CSV with subscription status and basic engagement metadata (last open date, last click date).
- Campaign templates as static HTML (visual styling may break — both platforms use proprietary block systems).
- Suppression lists.

What does not move:
- Automation logic (rebuild from scratch on the target platform).
- Segment definitions (the underlying schemas differ — predicted-CLV segments on Klaviyo have no direct Omnisend equivalent).
- Engagement scores or predictive labels.
- Consent history beyond opt-in date.

Expected timeline: 1–2 weeks at sub-10k contacts including deliverability warm-up; 3–4 weeks at 10–25k. The warm-up matters — both platforms will throttle new accounts during the first send batches to protect IP reputation.

## Our recommendation

**Choose Omnisend if:**
- You're under 5,000 active subscribers and want predictable cheaper math.
- You'd actually use MCP — meaning you want ChatGPT-style queries against your own marketing data.
- You send meaningful SMS volume and want bundled credits, not per-message overage.
- You're optimizing for fast setup and adequate (not category-leading) ecommerce flows.

**Choose Klaviyo if:**
- You're past 5,000 active profiles AND your list is generating revenue at $0.80+ per subscriber per month.
- You need browse abandonment, predicted CLV, or any behavioral flow that hinges on event-level granularity.
- You're already paying $300+/mo on support and Customer Agent's $200/mo would replace equivalent VA or helpdesk spend.
- You want a single platform that scales from 5k to 250k contacts without changing tools.

**Tie-breaker (the one thing that flips the decision).** If your list has a significant dormant-but-not-suppressed segment, Klaviyo's active-profile billing is going to surprise you. Until you've run list hygiene (sunset flows, suppression sweeps), Klaviyo's bill will look 30–50% higher than the headline tier suggests. If you don't want to commit to ongoing list hygiene as a recurring monthly task, Omnisend's subscribed-contacts denominator is the lower-stress choice — even at higher per-contact prices.
