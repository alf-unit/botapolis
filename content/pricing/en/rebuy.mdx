Rebuy's pricing in 2026 is structured around two ideas — **package-based à la carte at the low end** (Build Your Plan from $25/mo) and **Platform One bundling at $534/mo base** for brands wanting everything. The structural pricing story most operators don't fully internalize is the **Rebuy-Generated Revenue (RGR) marginal scaling above Enterprise tier**: $249 per additional $10K RGR means the platform cost climbs precisely when Rebuy is working. The ROI Guarantee mitigates downside risk; understanding the marginal cost ceiling matters more for budgeting.

This piece does the math at the bands where Shopify mid-market and enterprise brands actually evaluate Rebuy ($5K-$200K monthly RGR) using rates verified 2026-06-03 against [rebuyengine.com/pricing](https://www.rebuyengine.com/pricing).

## Bottom line up front

- **Best for:** Shopify and Shopify Plus stores with diverse SKUs (10+) wanting to lift AOV/LTV via cart, checkout, and PDP personalization.
- **Avoid if:** Single-SKU or very small catalog stores, brands without paid traffic, teams unwilling to configure rules/widgets.
- **Standout strength:** Smart Cart documented AOV lift + named CSM support model + ROI Guarantee with 60-day refund window.
- **Biggest weakness:** Package-based stacking compounds quickly + RGR marginal scaling above $40K + weekend support lag at sub-Platform-One tiers.

## What the pricing model gets wrong

Three things, in order of bill impact:

**1. Package-based stacking opacity.** Operators evaluating Build Your Plan can't easily model the full-stack cost without engaging the sales team. A "select your packages + enter your OPM" calculator on the pricing page would reduce evaluation friction.

**2. Steep Grow tier at $749/mo with limited mid-tier alternative.** Mid-market brands outgrowing Build Your Plan but not yet at Platform One scale find the jump material. The Build Your Plan model partially solves this (pay only for needed packages) but the $749 Grow tier remains a friction point for brands that need depth beyond Build Your Plan but not Platform One scale.

**3. Enterprise RGR marginal scaling without visible cap.** $249 per additional $10K RGR has no published ceiling. At $500K RGR, the marginal accumulation becomes substantial ($12K+/mo). Enterprise tier customers should negotiate the marginal rate at signature, not accept the published default.

## When the math doesn't work

Three honest scenarios where Rebuy doesn't pencil out:

- **Single-SKU or very small catalog stores.** Smart Cart, Bundle Builder, and Smart Search require catalog depth. A brand with 1-3 SKUs has limited upsell/cross-sell surface, and Rebuy's tools find no leverage. LimeSpot's flat-rate model fits better at this scale.
- **Brands without paid traffic.** Rebuy's value is in lifting AOV on traffic you're already paying to acquire. Brands relying purely on organic/SEO traffic with low traffic volume don't have enough sessions to meaningfully lift via Rebuy.
- **Teams unwilling to configure rules/widgets.** Smart Cart's value comes from configuration — selecting rules, segments, promotions. Brands that install and don't configure get a default Smart Cart that lifts AOV modestly; ROI scales with configuration depth.

## How to lower a Rebuy bill without losing capability

In rough order of impact:

1. **Start with Build Your Plan's Cart & Merchandising package only.** $25-$60/mo entry; validate Smart Cart AOV lift before expanding to additional packages.
2. **Use the ROI Guarantee 60-day window aggressively.** Track attribution rigorously during the first 60 days. If lift isn't material, exit cleanly.
3. **Annual billing saves 10%** ("Save 10% annually" per vendor page).
4. **Negotiate Enterprise RGR marginal rate at signature.** The published $249/$10K is the default; high-volume brands typically negotiate lower marginal rates.
5. **Audit package usage quarterly.** If Flows & A/B Testing module isn't being actively used, drop it. Build Your Plan's modular structure makes downsizing painless.
