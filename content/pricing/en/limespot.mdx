LimeSpot's pricing has consolidated around one plan: Max at $150/mo, tiered by your store's revenue, with a 50% lifetime discount promo that effectively halves it for new signups. The personalization itself — 1:1 AI product recommendations that lift AOV — is the value; the thing to understand before signing is the revenue-tiered structure and the auto-tier-bumping that comes with it.

This page covers the current Max pricing, how the revenue tiers and auto-bumping work, what's bundled, and where Rebuy is the better fit. Verified 2026-06-04 from limespot.com/pricing.

## Bottom line up front

- **Best for:** SMB-to-mid Shopify and BigCommerce brands with a real catalog (10+ SKUs) where cross-sell and upsell can lift AOV.
- **Avoid if:** you're single-SKU, an enterprise needing a full CDP, or off Shopify/BigCommerce.
- **Standout strength:** AI recommendations that measurably move AOV, all features bundled, free customization on install.
- **Biggest weakness:** revenue-tiered pricing with auto-tier-bumping, plus historical product-lineup confusion.

## The pricing in numbers (verified 2026-06-04)

| Plan | Monthly | Revenue band | What's included |
|---|---|---|---|
| Max | $150/mo (≈$75 with 50% lifetime promo) | up to $50,000 / 30 days | All features bundled, free customization on install |
| Higher tiers | Scales up | above $50K/30 days | Same features, higher revenue band |

There's a 15-day free trial. The structural fact: pricing is **revenue-tiered**, and the plan auto-bumps you to a higher tier when you cross your selected revenue threshold mid-cycle. Confirm you're on the current Max plan — LimeSpot has historically run overlapping products (Personalizer, Turbo) that muddy tier selection.

## How the revenue tiers and auto-bumping work

The Max plan is banded by 30-day online store revenue: $150/mo covers up to $50,000, with higher tiers above that. The mechanic to plan around is auto-tier-bumping — if your revenue exceeds the threshold mid-cycle, LimeSpot moves you up. For a steadily-growing brand that's predictable; for a seasonal one, a BFCM spike can push you into a higher tier for that period. Budget the band above your current one if you're trending up, and watch the threshold during peak months.

The 50% lifetime discount promo, when running, effectively halves the rate for new signups — confirm it's active at signup, because it materially changes the math (≈$75/mo instead of $150 at the entry band).

## What you get for the price

Everything is bundled into Max — no feature gating within the plan:

- **1:1 AI product recommendations** across home, PDP, cart, post-purchase, and order-status pages.
- **Real-time audience segmentation** by history, behavior, URL params, and location.
- **AI-built personalized bundles** for upsell, cross-sell, and post-purchase.
- **Per-segment personalized discounts** applied automatically.
- **Shapeshifting collections and content** that reorder based on visitor affinity.
- **Email and SMS personalization** injected into [Klaviyo](/tools/klaviyo) flows.
- **A/B/n multivariate testing** across the journey.
- **70+ app integrations** plus free customization on install.

The free-customization-on-install is a real differentiator — LimeSpot configures the setup for you rather than handing your team a blank canvas.

## When the math works — and when it doesn't

**It pays back when:**
- You have a real catalog (10+ SKUs) where cross-sell and upsell recommendations have something to work with.
- AOV lift from recommendations clears the $75–$150/mo cost — easy at meaningful order volume.
- You want personalization configured for you without a developer.

**It doesn't pay back when:**
- You're single-SKU or very small-catalog — the recommendation engine has little to recommend.
- You need a full enterprise CDP/personalization suite — Dynamic Yield or Bloomreach fit that scope.
- You're off Shopify and BigCommerce — LimeSpot's native depth doesn't apply.
